Branding Tips

Fuel Your Success With Your Powerful Brand

Logos

Your brand is not your logo

Your brand is not your logo.
Your brand is that gut feeling that people have about you.
Your brand is the story that they share with others about you.
Branding is controlling that story, that feeling.
Great branding does this with intent.
They decide their story and make sure that not only do you know it, but you believe it.



Your logo is irrelevant

It doesn’t matter what your logo is.
All your logo is simply a short cut to the story your brand tells.
If your not telling a story your logo doesn’t matter.
Your logo only means something to those people who have interacted with your brand, who you’ve had the opportunity to share your story with.
Your logo is used to connect them back to that story.
To trigger the emotions you want them to feel. To make them remember what you want them to believe.
Remember, nobody has ever bought a product because they had a fancy logo.
Could be almost anything you want. As long as your logo doesn’t conflict with the story you are telling.











Story

How to find your story

We want your story to resonate with your audience while being true to you.
Find the reason why you exist as a business, why this is important to you, what value you are trying to bring to people.
Find the story that your target audience wants to believe. What do they want to be true.
Your powerful story is found when your why meets their beliefs.

5 Minute Story


Be Coherent

With all of your actions, words, and graphics you need to be coherent.
Make sure you are telling the same story with everything you do.
I often see people guilty of conflicting their words with their actions.
For example, if you are creating a luxury brand:

  • Don’t have discounts.
  • Don’t use hard squeezing sales tactics.
  • These paint a conflicting story, that you are cheap and pushing a sale.
  • It is the opposite to what people who want luxury believe.
  • People who buy luxury don’t want to buy cheap or be pushed.
  • They want to be pampered and buy the expensive items. To feel a million dollars.

Make sure you are telling the story your audience wants to believe with all your words, all your actions, all your graphics.











Analyse Market Place

Look At Your Competition

Most of the time you want to tell a different story to your competition, though there are exceptions.
Think energy drinks, Redbull vs Monster. Both go for high energy and extreme.
But often it is better to tell a different story Think of soft drinks.
Coke, pepsi the anti coke, 7up was created as the not coke and sprite is cokes anti 7up.
And with all that noise it is often better to be the Lucazade.
Don’t just analyse your direct competition, but look at any indirect too.
What stories do they tell?
Instead of soft drinks, you have:
Coffee, fruit juices, smoothies, etc...
And not just drinks, but things like Ice Cream are competition too. Think of hot summer summer day.
Sprite or an ice cream?



Positioning

Even better if you can position yourself away from your competition.
Instead of putting chocolates in a corner shop.
Sell luxury chocolates for 2x the price in a flower shop. Flowers and chocolate goes hand in hand.
And if you are already spending 20 quid of flowers whats a few more on some chocolate.
Positioning is vital.











User Experience

How do you want them to feel?

How do you want them to feel?
What do you want them to think?
Controlling your tone and voice sets the vibe and the emotional impact.
Choose what you want them to feel and think. Decide in advance.
Don’t just hope they will get it.
Make sure they feel it.
Make sure they know it.
Feed them with what you want them to believe.
Don’t leave it to chance.



Identity

People are craving to tell the world a story.
They want to show the world who they are.
To belong to a specific group of people who believe the same as they believe.
Facilitate that story, and they will line up to buy from you.
